03 · Revenue impact

CLIM Revenue Loss

Only rooms labelled CLIM (air conditioning works) are counted. Period: June 1–20, 2026. Rates applied: Ocean / Ocean Balcony / Ocean Garden £155 · Ocean Balcony Big £188 · Standard £105.
CLIM blocks · room by room · Jun 1–20, 2026
Room Category CLIM nights Rate / night Lost revenue
Total · 17 rooms affected

What this means

All 62 CLIM nights were concentrated in June 9–20. Running AC works across 17 rooms simultaneously during peak season removed a significant portion of sellable inventory at the busiest time of year.

Recommendation

Staggering AC works over the low season (November–January), room by room, would have avoided this loss entirely. All non-emergency maintenance should be scheduled within the low season window going forward.

05 · Analysis

Key Findings

Critical

Ocean rooms 01–03 show genuine booking gaps from mid-May onward — not CLIM, not blocked. These were fully occupied through Q2 2025. The shortfall is a real demand or pricing issue at the highest per-night revenue rate.

Critical

62 CLIM nights across 17 rooms concentrated in June 9–20 represent £8,454 in directly avoidable revenue loss. Running AC works on 17 rooms simultaneously during peak season is a scheduling decision with a quantifiable and preventable cost.

High

Ocean Garden Big rooms 34–36 are the most blocked room type in Q2 2026 — a combination of CLIM and other blocks resulted in a 34-point gap versus last year, the largest of any category.

High

Standard rooms 16–22, while down 16 points YoY, are holding the best relative performance of any non-bunk room type. Demand for accessible price points is more resilient than premium inventory.

Positive

April 2026 is only 4 points behind April 2025 — underlying property demand is intact. The deterioration is concentrated in May and June, pointing to a pricing, booking-window, or supply-side problem rather than a structural demand issue.

Positive

Room 15 Bunk held at approximately 80% through Q2 — the most consistent performer in the property across both years. Demand for shared beds at Amouage is strong and reliable.

Note

Bookings are visible immediately before and after CLIM periods on most affected rooms, confirming guests would have filled them had they been available. The CLIM loss is opportunity cost, not a lack of demand.
